On the Subject of The Great Void

"I have to click SIX TIMES in ONE SECOND?!"

On the module, there is a rotating black sphere with six colored segments. Each segment has a number from 0 to 6 on it.

Firstly, identify the first face. This face is the one with the black dot. This black dot is "up" for the module.

Next, identify the color and number of each face, in order. The face order is in the table below.

2
5146
3

You may have noticed each number is encrypted. After this sentence are the numbers from 0-6, when they are "up". 0123456

Each color also has its own number. In order, from 0 to 6, they are: Red, Green, Blue, Magenta, Yellow, Cyan, and White.

Using the color as the column and the number as the row, find your position in the center 7x7 table, where the top left is 0,0.

You need to obtain 3 digits for each face. The first is the position you are on. The second is obtained by moving up 1, rotating n times, where n is the face number, then moving forward one, and summing those 2 digits, mod 3. The third is the same process, moving 'forward' 1, rotating, then moving forward 2, and summing all of those digits, mod 3.

However, if you touch the edge, you must 'bounce' off of it. If you hit it straight on, reverse your direction. If you hit it at an angle, reverse the direction like a mirror. If you reach a gray space, teleport to the gray space on the other side of the grid.

Now that you have obtained all the digits for all 6 faces, we now need to reorder them.

If... Then...
1's symbol is greater than 4's, swap the digits of 2 and 3.
5's symbol is greater than 2's, swap the digits of 4 and 6.
3's symbol is greater than 6's, swap the digits of 5 and 1.
3 is red, cycle the digits 1 left (1 gets 2...).
6 is green, reverse the sequence.
1 is blue, swap 1 and 2, 3 and 4, and 5 and 6.

Now that we have the correct digits in the correct order, we need to turn them into presses for the module.

Make the first digit of the sequence a '1'.

Use the table (on the next page) to convert you numbers into a sequence of [,], and ps.

Now that you have your input sequence, you must input it. '[' means hold, ']' means release, and 'p' means pulse. When you start inputting, the void will pulse white every second. Completing this input correctly will solve the module.
